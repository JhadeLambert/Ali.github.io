El Yunque National Forest, Puerto Rico

Details

Located on the Caribbean Island of Puerto Rico in the Greater Antilles group, the El Yunque National Forest is the sole tropical rain forest in the U.S. National Forest System. The forest’s relatively small 28,516 acre size belies its importance. It differs significantly from all the other U.S. National Forests because of its year-round tropical climate and immense biodiversity.

Discussion of the Place

The rugged Luquillo Mountains that rise around three thousand and a half ft. which is above sea level that comprise most of the forest land. The steep slopes of the forest can possibly receive rainfall of around two hundred inches in each year resuting to higher elevations. The average temperature of forest was measure 73 degrees farenheit and its seasonal changes are almost impossible to know. It is said that it is the ideal climate for vegetation. The forest is notable for its biodiversity with various types of plants living in the forest that are around 150 fern species, 240 tree species and other that is exclusive or can only be found in the specified forest. The El Yunque National Forest has found no signs of large animals, but found a hundred of smaller animals living inside the gentle forest, that can also only be found in the forest.

How to get there

After a flight to the country head to the Río Grande area, east of San Juan, via PR-66 to PR-3 and then following PR-191. Traveling to El Yunque from San Juan will take you approximately 45 minutes. Before the pandemic, the forest was available as a tourist spot followed by a few fees on the specified loactions of the forest. Reservations are also possible with an ID, time and other information important to the specified need when reserving.
